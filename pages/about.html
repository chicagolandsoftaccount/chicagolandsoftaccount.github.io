About Chicagoland Software

Alan is a software developer in Chicago who has also lived in St. Louis.

Education

Alan studied IT at College of DuPage before transferring to Southern Illinois University Edwardsville to study computer science. He then moved back to Chicago to be closer to his relatives, and is currently finishing his computer science degree at NEIU, located on Chicago's north side. He studied abroad through the SIUE School of Business and went to Shanghai, Hangzhou, and Beijing. He has also gone to hackathons and created many apps and websites.

Skills

Alan's best skills are Java, Python, MySQL, Git, Bash, Linux, Wordpress, and Bootstrap. He is currently learning more about PHP and server-side development.

Future learning goals and flexibility

In the future, Alan wants to learn about Amazon Web Services, containers, container orchestration, MVC, automatic testing and deployment, more advanced git commands, and in-depth details about web application firewalls, as well as how to protect against OWASP vulnerability categories. These are concepts he is vaguely aware of but not well-versed in. This will mostly be independent learning. But in college, he will continue to learn core CS concepts.

That being said, he can also learn whatever tech stack is required for a job.

Nowadays, tech skills shouldn't be set in stone. Technology changes all the time, so it's important to be able to adapt to industry changes. It's extremely important to be able to quicky learn new languages, frameworks, and tools.
